\section*{The one-line pitch}
The state has built big digital systems (education portal, health systems, forest tools), but many officers still rely on paper, WhatsApp, and Excel. We do not really know how big that gap is, or why it happens.

\section*{What you want to do}
A small, focused study: talk to officers in \textbf{School Education, Health, and Forest} about what they actually use each day, and talk to their \textbf{IT teams} about what they built and what problems they see. You are not auditing individuals---you are mapping \textbf{real practice versus official tools}.

\section*{How you keep it practical}
Use simple questions: daily tasks, apps or websites used, why the official system is hard, what training they got. Sort barriers into clear buckets---\textbf{skill, awareness, access, motivation, process}---so the report is easy to act on.

\section*{What the organisation gets}
A short report with: (1)~who uses what, (2)~where gaps sit by department and level, and (3)~\textbf{concrete recommendations} for AIGGPA, especially training and handholding that match real work, not generic computer classes.

\section*{Why they should approve}
It turns ``we have portals'' into ``here is what blocks use and what to fix first.'' It supports better use of money already spent on IT and fits AIGGPA's role in \textbf{capacity building}.

\section*{The 30-second version (if time is short)}
``We've invested in digital systems, but field use looks weak. I want to interview officers and IT staff in three departments to see what they really use and why they skip official tools, then give AIGGPA a simple gap map and training recommendations---so we fix real blockers, not guess.''

\section*{Closing line}
``I'm asking for access and time to interview a limited set of people over April--June, then deliver one clear report AIGGPA can use with departments.''
