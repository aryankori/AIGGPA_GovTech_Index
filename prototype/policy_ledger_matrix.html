Executive Summary

The "Architectural Ledger" approach to policy analysis demands a shift away from qualitative sentiment toward quantitative geometric mapping. By charting "Digitisation Maturity" against "Public Interaction," we observe a distinct stratification in administrative performance.

Quadrants I and II represent the aspirational state of institutional governance. The Revenue department serves as the primary benchmark, exhibiting a 1:1 correlation between backend technological investment and public fiscal engagement. This model suggests that maturity is not merely an internal metric but a catalyst for interaction.

Conversely, the Education sector, while exhibiting moderate interaction, lacks the backend digitisation required to scale institutional efficacy. The ledger indicates a need for immediate infrastructure pivot.
