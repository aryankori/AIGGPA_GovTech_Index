\vspace*{0.2cm}
\begin{goldbox}
  \textbf{\color{navyblue}\large Executive Summary}\\[6pt]
  Madhya Pradesh has deployed major digital governance platforms across its departments
  -- Education Portal 3.0, e-Office, HRMIS, SPARROW, MPOnline -- yet a persistent gap
  exists between \textit{tool deployment} and \textit{actual day-to-day adoption}.
  This proposal outlines a structured field study to document what IT tools officials
  are really using, how they use them, and where the gaps are. Findings will directly
  inform AIGGPA's capacity building interventions.
\end{goldbox}

\vspace{0.4cm}

\section{Background}

\vspace{0.2cm}
Government departments in Madhya Pradesh operate a growing suite of digital tools.
The Education Portal 3.0 (Figure 1) integrates Human Resource Management, School
Management, Dashboards, and Budget Monitoring for over 350,000 teachers across
90,000+ schools. The Health Department operates HRMIS, NHM CHETNA, and the
AI-powered Ayushman Sakhi helpline. The Forest Department uses satellite-based
encroachment detection across five pilot divisions and manages all wildlife tourism
via MPOnline.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig3_education_portal.png}
\captionsetup{justification=raggedright,singlelinecheck=false}
\caption{\small\textit{\textbf{Figure 1.} MP Education Portal 3.0 showing 12 integrated management modules.
  School Education Department, Government of Madhya Pradesh (2025).}}
\end{figure}

Despite this investment, officials at district and block levels continue to use manual
registers, physical dak, and informal channels like WhatsApp for tasks these platforms
were built to automate. This study asks: who is using what, and why are the gaps there?

% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
%  RESEARCH OBJECTIVES
% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\section{Research Objectives}

\vspace{0.2cm}
\begin{navybox}
\begin{enumerate}[label={\color{gold}\bfseries\arabic*.},itemsep=4pt]
  \item Map the daily roles of officials and identify which functions have an IT tool available.
  \item Document IT tools \textbf{actually in use}: official platforms and informal
    substitutes (Excel, WhatsApp, Google Sheets).
  \item Understand how officials \textbf{draft, type, and process} official letters
    and communications.
  \item Engage each department's \textbf{IT cell} to map tools provided, training
    delivered, and adoption levels observed from the IT side.
  \item Classify gaps by type: \textbf{Skill, Awareness, Availability, Motivation,}
    or \textbf{Process} -- and generate targeted recommendations.
\end{enumerate}
\end{navybox}
