\section{Background}
%----------------------------------------------------------

\begin{onehalfspacing}
Madhya Pradesh has invested significantly in digital governance infrastructure -- from the Education Portal 2.0 to e-Office, HRMIS, and the Forest Department's AI-based alert system. However, the deployment of a digital tool does not automatically imply its adoption. Across government departments, officials at the state and district level continue to manage critical workflows -- letter drafting, leave management, data reporting, and internal approvals -- through manual processes, despite the existence of IT tools designed for these exact functions.

AIGGPA, as the apex institution for governance and policy capacity building in MP, has a mandate to identify and bridge such gaps. This study aims to conduct a structured field assessment across selected departments to understand the ground-level reality of IT utilisation by officials in their day-to-day roles.
\end{onehalfspacing}

\vspace{0.2cm}

%----------------------------------------------------------
\section{Research Objectives}
%----------------------------------------------------------

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{O\arabic*.}]
  \item Map the daily roles and responsibilities of officials at various levels and identify which of those roles can be supported by IT tools.
  \item Document the IT tools currently used by officials -- formally mandated tools as well as informal tools (Excel, WhatsApp, Google Docs).
  \item Assess the IT tools and support systems deployed by the department's IT unit and evaluate their reach to end-users.
  \item Identify gaps between available IT tools and actual utilisation, and classify the nature of those gaps (skill deficit, hardware unavailability, awareness gap, or cultural resistance).
  \item Generate actionable recommendations for AIGGPA's capacity building programmes.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Methodology}
%----------------------------------------------------------

The study employs an \textbf{exploratory qualitative-quantitative mixed approach} combining semi-structured interviews with a structured questionnaire. The field engagement is divided into two distinct respondent streams.

\subsection{Stream A -- Department Officials and Employees \hindi{(विभागीय अधिकारी एवं कर्मचारी)}}

Officials across grades (Class I, II, and III) will be questioned on:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Their primary role and key daily tasks -- what does their job require them to do?
  \item Which of those tasks currently involve IT tools, and which do not?
  \item What specific IT tools they use -- e.g., e-Office, HRMIS, dashboards, Excel, WhatsApp?
  \item Whether they use IT tools for drafting and typing letters or official communications, and how.
  \item If they do \textit{not} use a relevant IT tool -- why not? (No training, no hardware, no internet, no awareness, or preference for manual method?)
  \item Where and how they learned to use the IT tools they currently use (formal training, self-taught, colleague assistance).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Stream B -- IT Department / IT Cell \hindi{(आईटी विभाग / आईटी प्रकोष्ठ)}}

The designated IT unit within each department will be interviewed on:

\begin{itemize}
  \item What IT tools and platforms the IT department itself manages and operates.
  \item What support mechanisms are in place to assist other employees in using digital tools.
  \item Whether the IT department has internally developed tools, dashboards, or platforms unique to the department.
  \item How IT support requests are handled -- helpdesk, on-site, or remote.
  \item Their assessment of employee adoption levels and the barriers they observe.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Gap Identification Framework}

After data collection, gaps will be classified under the following categories:

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=lightgray,colframe=govblue!40,arc=2mm,boxrule=0.4pt]
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\textbf{Gap Type} & \textbf{Description} \\[4pt]
Skill Gap & Tool exists but official lacks the competency to use it \\
Availability Gap & Tool mandated but hardware, software, or internet not provided \\
Awareness Gap & Official unaware that the tool exists or is relevant to their role \\
Motivation Gap & Tool available and known, but official prefers manual method \\
Process Gap & IT tool exists but it does not map to the actual workflow of the official \\
\end{tabular}
\end{tcolorbox}

%----------------------------------------------------------
\section{Data Collection Instruments}
%----------------------------------------------------------

Two bilingual (English/Hindi) instruments have been prepared:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Structured Questionnaire for Officials} -- face-to-face survey, approximately 10--12 minutes.
  \item \textbf{Semi-Structured Interview Guide for IT Department} -- open-ended, approximately 20--30 minutes.
\end{enumerate}

Data will be recorded via a mobile-based digital form (already developed) to enable real-time field entry.

\vspace{0.4cm}

%----------------------------------------------------------
\section{Expected Output}
%----------------------------------------------------------

\begin{itemize}
  \item A \textbf{department-wise IT utilisation map} showing which roles use which tools.
  \item A \textbf{gap classification matrix} identifying the nature and location of IT adoption gaps.
  \item A concise \textbf{report with recommendations} for AIGGPA, usable for designing targeted capacity building interventions.
\end{itemize}
