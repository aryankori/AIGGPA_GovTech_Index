\section{Background}

Madhya Pradesh has deployed a range of digital governance tools across departments -- Education Portal 2.0, e-Office, HRMIS, SPARROW, and department-specific management systems. However, deployment does not guarantee use. A gap routinely observed in government offices is that IT tools exist on paper but employees -- particularly at district and block levels -- continue to rely on manual methods for functions those tools were built to support. This study proposes a focused field assessment to understand who is using what, who is not, and why.

\section{Research Objectives}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Identify the key daily roles of officials across departments and determine which roles have corresponding IT tools available.
  \item Document IT tools actually in use -- both mandated platforms and informal substitutes such as Excel or WhatsApp.
  \item Understand how officials draft and process official letters and communications.
  \item Engage each department's IT cell to map tools provided, training offered, and internal adoption estimates.
  \item Identify and classify gaps in IT adoption by type: skill, awareness, availability, motivation, or process gap.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Methodology}

The study uses face-to-face structured interviews at department offices, split into two respondent streams.

\textit{Stream A -- Department officials:} Each official is asked about their daily responsibilities, which tasks they use IT tools for, how they draft official letters, what tools they use on dashboards or for data reporting, and -- if they do not use relevant tools -- why not and where they learned any tools they do use.

\textit{Stream B -- IT department staff:} IT cell officers are asked what tools and platforms they manage, how they support other employees, whether any tools are built internally, and what they estimate current adoption levels to be across the department.

Responses will be recorded on a mobile digital form. After each visit, the researcher will classify the primary gap observed (skill, awareness, availability, motivation, or process) to build a systematised gap map across all three departments (Davis, 1989; Tornatzky \& Fleischer, 1990).

\section{Expected Output}

\begin{itemize}
  \item A department-wise map of IT tools in use versus tools available, by role and level.
  \item A classified gap analysis identifying where and why adoption is falling short.
  \item A concise report with targeted recommendations for AIGGPA's capacity building programmes.
\end{itemize}
