# Pitch Script — Convincing Your Manager to Fund the GovTech Adoption Index

> **Format:** 10-minute presentation. Read this like a speaking guide — the **bold text** is what you say, the *italic text* is what you do.

---

## OPENING (90 seconds)

*Stand. Make eye contact. No slides yet.*

**"Sir/Ma'am, I've spent the first few days doing desk research on HR-IT integration across government. I found something interesting — and a problem worth solving."**

**"The Government of India has built world-class platforms — e-Office, e-HRMS, iGOT Karmayogi, GeM, PFMS. iGOT alone has 1.53 crore registered users. But here's the gap:"**

*Pause for effect.*

**"Nobody has measured whether the people in THIS building, in THESE departments, are actually using them. We know the platforms exist. We don't know if they're being used — or why they're not."**

**"I want to build a diagnostic tool to measure that gap. I'm calling it the GovTech Adoption Index."**

---

## THE FRAMEWORK (2 minutes)

*Open the prototype dashboard on your laptop — Overview page.*

**"Every government employee falls into one of three tiers:"**

*Point to the three tier cards on screen.*

**"Tier 1 — Active Adopters. They use e-Office daily, file digitally, complete iGOT courses. These are your success stories."**

**"Tier 2 — Hesitant Users. They KNOW about the tools, they may have been trained, but they still default to paper. They print e-files for physical signatures. This is the biggest fixable gap."**

**"Tier 3 — Unaware Non-Users. They don't even know these platforms exist. No training, no access, sometimes no computer."**

**"The Index measures the distribution across these three tiers — per department, per grade, per age group — and gives each department a score out of 100."**

---

## THE DEMO (3 minutes)

*Click through the prototype:*

1. *Show the Survey Form page* — **"This is the data collection instrument. Bilingual — Hindi and English. It captures 6 demographic variables and 15 metrics, including Likert-scale perceptions."**

2. *Click 'Load Demo Data'* — **"I've pre-loaded 120 simulated responses across 4 departments."**

3. *Navigate to Analytics* — **"Here's what the output looks like. Revenue scores 72, Education scores 30. This is hypothetical, but THIS is what we'd produce with real data."**

4. *Show the radar chart* — **"We can compare confidence, frustration, willingness across departments — and identify exactly WHERE the intervention is needed."**

5. *Navigate to Data Store* — **"Everything is exportable. CSV for Excel, ready for SPSS analysis."**

---

## THE ASK (2 minutes)

**"To make this real, I need three things from you:"**

**"One — access to 4 departments. I'm recommending Revenue, PWD, Health, and Education because they span the full digitisation spectrum."**

**"Two — permission to survey approximately 30 employees per department. That's 120 total. A structured questionnaire, 15 minutes each."**

**"Three — access to any internal data you can share. e-Office login frequencies, iGOT completion rates, hardware inventories. Even partial data makes the Index more credible."**

**"In return, you get a policy-ready diagnostic report with a composite score for each department, a clear identification of barriers, and actionable recommendations."**

---

## CLOSING (90 seconds)

**"This isn't just an internship project. MoSPI just open-sourced an MCP server for their statistical data. NHAI is building AI-powered data analytics. The government is moving toward AI agents that can query institutional data in natural language."**

**"If AIGGPA has its own data pipeline — even a prototype — it positions the institute as a leader in evidence-based governance research. Not just in MP, but nationally."**

**"I can deliver the first version in 12 weeks. The framework, the data, the analysis, and the report. All I need is your go-ahead."**

*Stop talking. Wait for their response.*

## HANDLING OBJECTIONS

| Objection | Your Response |
|-----------|--------------|
| "We don't have budget for this" | "This costs nothing — free tools (KoboToolbox, Python, LaTeX). The only resource I need is access and your permission." |
| "Why these 4 departments?" | "They span the full digitisation spectrum — high to low — which gives us maximum comparative insight from minimum sampling." |
| "120 people isn't enough" | "For a diagnostic pilot, 120 is standard. It's enough for cross-tabulation by 6 demographic variables. If the pilot proves valuable, it can be scaled to all departments." |
| "Why not use existing data?" | "If you can give me e-Office login data or iGOT completion rates, I'll use them. The survey captures what logs CAN'T — perceptions, barriers, and the 'why' behind non-adoption." |
| "What's MCP? Why AI agents?" | "MoSPI just launched one — it lets AI tools query government statistics in natural language. If AIGGPA builds one, researchers and policymakers can ask questions of your data without needing a data analyst." |
