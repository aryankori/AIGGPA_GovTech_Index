Assessment of the Use of Digital Tools and Technologies by Government Employees for Enhancing Workplace Efficiency and Effectiveness

Intern Research Framework — AIGGPA, Bhopal | April 2026

1

Digital Infrastructure, Awareness & UsageResearch Objective 1

Research Question
What is the current status of digital infrastructure in the selected departments, and to what extent are government employees aware of and using various digital tools and technologies in their daily office work?
Indicators
① Number & type of digital devices available per employee
② Internet connectivity (bandwidth & uptime)
③ Awareness level of digital tools (% aware)
④ Frequency of daily digital tool usage
⑤ Types of tools used (e-Office, email, portals, etc.)
Quantitative / Qualitative
Mixed

Quantitative: device counts, usage frequency scores

Qualitative: observational notes from office visits
Analysis Tools
• Descriptive statistics (frequency, mean, SD)
• Cross-tabulation by department & cadre
• TAM "Facilitating Conditions" construct mapping
Visual Representation
① Bar chart — device availability by department
② Heatmap — tool usage frequency across cadres
③ Stacked bar — awareness levels by age group
Levels
Head Office +
District Office
(Both)

All 4 Departments
Schedule / Questionnaire Questions
Q1. What digital devices (computer, tablet, smartphone) are available at your workstation?

Q2. How would you rate the internet connectivity at your office? (5-point Likert)

Q3. Which of the following digital tools are you aware of? [Checklist: e-Office, CPGRAMS, email, dept. portal, etc.]

Q4. How frequently do you use digital tools in your daily work? (Daily / Weekly / Rarely / Never)

Q5. (Observation) Document physical infrastructure conditions during office visit.
Means of Verification
① Direct observation during office visits
② Cross-referencing with department IT inventory records
③ Triangulation between survey responses and observational data

Capacity Building & Training AssessmentResearch Objective 2

Research Question
What kind of capacity building efforts, training programs, and technical support are being provided by the departments to help government employees use digital tools effectively?
Indicators
① Number of training programs conducted (last 2 years)
② % of employees who attended formal IT training
③ Duration & content of training programs
④ Availability of on-site IT helpdesk/support
⑤ Employee satisfaction with training quality (Likert)
Quantitative / Qualitative
Mixed

Quantitative: training frequency, attendance %, satisfaction score

Qualitative: semi-structured interview narratives on training quality
Analysis Tools
• Descriptive statistics
• Likert-scale analysis (mean, median)
• Thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke, 2006)
• UTAUT "Effort Expectancy" & "Social Influence" mapping
Visual Representation
① Grouped bar chart — training programs by department
② Pie chart — % employees trained vs. untrained
③ Radar/spider chart — training quality dimensions (content, duration, relevance, follow-up)
Levels
Head Office +
District Office
(Both)

All 4 Departments
Schedule / Questionnaire Questions
Q6. Have you received any formal training on digital tools from your department? (Yes/No)

Q7. If yes, how many training sessions have you attended in the last 2 years?

Q8. How would you rate the quality and relevance of the training? (5-point Likert)

Q9. Is there an IT helpdesk or technical support person available in your office? (Yes/No)

Q10. (Interview) "Can you describe your experience with the training you received? What was useful and what was missing?"
Means of Verification
① Department training records / circulars
② Mission Karmayogi (iGOT) enrolment data
③ Triangulation: employee responses vs. official training records

(To be filled after fieldwork)

Bottlenecks, Issues & ChallengesResearch Objective 3

Research Question
What are the major bottlenecks, issues, and challenges faced by government employees while using digital tools and technologies in their work?
Indicators
① Types of technical issues reported (connectivity, hardware, software)
② Frequency of system downtime
③ Perceived difficulty of using digital tools (Effort Expectancy)
④ Attitudinal barriers (resistance to change, digital anxiety)
⑤ Organizational barriers (lack of mandate, no accountability)
Quantitative / Qualitative
Mixed

Quantitative: issue frequency, Likert scales on perceived difficulty

Qualitative: in-depth interview narratives, FGD themes
Analysis Tools
• Thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke, 2006) — primary method
• Frequency analysis of reported issues
• TAM/UTAUT construct mapping (Effort Expectancy, Facilitating Conditions)
• Ranking analysis for prioritising challenges
Visual Representation
① Pareto chart — top bottlenecks ranked by frequency
② Word cloud — qualitative themes from interviews
③ Diverging bar chart — Likert responses on difficulty levels
④ Fishbone/Ishikawa diagram — root cause categorization
Levels
Head Office +
District Office
(Both)

All 4 Departments
All Cadres (I–IV)
Schedule / Questionnaire Questions
Q11. What are the biggest challenges you face when using digital tools at work? (Open-ended)

Q12. How often do you experience technical issues (system crashes, slow internet, software errors)? (Daily / Weekly / Monthly / Rarely)

Q13. How difficult do you find the current digital tools to use? (5-point Likert)

Q14. Do you feel adequately supported when you face technical problems? (Yes / Somewhat / No)

Q15. (FGD) "What systemic changes would make digital tools easier to use in your daily work?"
Means of Verification
① Triangulation across multiple data sources (survey + interview + FGD)
② Observation of actual workflow disruptions during office visits
③ Cross-referencing with IT complaint/ticket logs (if available)

Recommendations for ImprovementResearch Objective 4

Research Question
What actions and measures can be suggested to improve the use of digital tools for more efficient and effective service delivery in government departments?
Indicators
① Priority areas for improvement (ranked by employees)
② Specific tool/software recommendations
③ Training needs assessment score
④ Infrastructure upgrade requirements
⑤ Policy-level recommendations count
Quantitative / Qualitative
Primarily Qualitative

Synthesised from findings of Objectives 1–3, supported by quantitative gap analysis
Analysis Tools
• Gap analysis (current state vs. desired state)
• Priority matrix
• Synthesis of thematic findings from Obj 1–3
• Benchmarking against OECD (2014) & UN DESA (2024) standards
Visual Representation
① Priority matrix (Impact vs. Feasibility)
② Recommendation summary table
③ Before-after comparison framework
④ Roadmap timeline (short / medium / long-term actions)
Levels
Department-level
&
State-level
(Policy recommendations)
Schedule / Questionnaire Questions
Q16. What specific improvements would help you use digital tools more effectively? (Open-ended)

Q17. If given a choice, which ONE area would you prioritise for improvement? (Infrastructure / Training / Software / Support / Policy)

Q18. (Interview) "In an ideal scenario, how would you like digital tools to support your work?"

Q19. (FGD) "What policy changes do you believe are most urgently needed?"
Means of Verification
① Evidence-based — all recommendations traced to findings from Obj 1–3
② Alignment with national frameworks (Digital India, Mission Karmayogi)
③ Feasibility check against budget and institutional constraints
